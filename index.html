Guide
How Market Whisperer Works
A plain-English explanation of everything this agent does, step by step.
1
It reads prediction markets Jupiter API
Prediction markets are like bet pools where people put real money on questions like "Will SOL reach $200 this month?" The higher the YES%, the more people think it will happen.

The agent checks these every 15 minutes — but it doesn't just look at the number. It watches for shifts. If something jumps from 40% to 65% YES in a few hours, that's the crowd suddenly getting very confident. That sudden change is the real signal.
2
It checks if the token is actually healthy Jupiter Tokens API
Sometimes prediction markets can be wrong or manipulated. So the agent also checks the token's "organic score" — basically, is the trading activity real or are bots faking it? It also checks how many real people hold the token, and whether any whale (very large holder) could dump and crash the price.

If whales own too much, the agent caps how much it buys regardless of the signal.
3
Claude AI combines everything into a score Claude claude-sonnet-4-6
All the data gets sent to Claude (Anthropic's AI), which thinks about it like a smart analyst would: "The crowd is getting more confident AND the token looks healthy AND volume is increasing — that's a strong buy signal." It gives a score from 0 to 100, and explains its reasoning in plain English. You can see the full explanation on the AI Reasoning page.
4
It adjusts how much SOL it buys Jupiter Recurring API
DCA means "Dollar Cost Averaging" — buying a fixed amount regularly. Like buying $35 of SOL every 4 hours. Market Whisperer makes this smarter: when the signal is bullish (score above 65), it buys more — like $50 or $60 instead of $35. When the signal is bearish (score below 30), it pauses completely and saves your money.

You still DCA, but you buy more when the crowd is confident and less when they're scared.
5
Every buy gets automatic profit-taking and loss protection Jupiter Trigger API
OCO stands for "One Cancels Other." Every time the agent buys SOL, it automatically places two orders:

• Take Profit — sell automatically if the price goes up by ~10%
• Stop Loss — sell automatically if the price drops by ~6%

When one triggers, the other cancels. This means you don't have to watch the market 24/7. The agent protects your money while letting winners run. The ratio is always set so potential gains are bigger than potential losses.
6
The actual swap happens on Solana Jupiter Swap V2
Jupiter finds the best price for your swap across dozens of exchanges on Solana automatically. You always get the best rate, with minimal fees. Gasless swaps are supported too.
